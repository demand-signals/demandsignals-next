Google Search Central releases what may be their most practically useful video for the majority of businesses: a focused guide to SEO specifically for small businesses. Rather than applying enterprise-scale SEO frameworks to small operations, the video identifies the highest-impact, lowest-effort tactics that small businesses should prioritize.

Watch the full video: [SEO for small businesses](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BKDymFYpvL8)

## Small Business SEO Is Different

The video begins with an important framing: small business SEO is fundamentally different from enterprise SEO. Small businesses typically have fewer pages, smaller budgets, limited technical resources, and a local or regional customer base. The SEO tactics that work for a site with millions of pages and a dedicated technical team are often irrelevant or counterproductive for a small business with a 20-page website.

Google's advice centers on a principle of focused effort. Rather than trying to optimize everything, small businesses should concentrate on the specific actions that deliver the greatest return for their investment of time and money. The video identifies these high-impact areas and provides actionable guidance for each.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Google Business Profile is your highest priority.** For small businesses serving local customers, Google Business Profile (GBP) is more important than website SEO. A complete, accurate, and actively maintained GBP listing directly influences whether your business appears in local search results, Google Maps, and the local pack. The video recommends completing every field, adding photos regularly, responding to reviews promptly, and posting updates weekly.

2. **Your website needs to do just a few things well.** Small business websites do not need hundreds of pages. They need clear service descriptions, accurate contact information, location details, and trust signals like reviews and credentials. A 10-page website that does these things well will outperform a 100-page site with thin, unfocused content.

3. **Write about what you actually know.** Google's emphasis on E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) particularly benefits small business owners who have deep expertise in their field. A plumber who writes detailed, experience-based content about common plumbing issues will outrank generic content from a content mill because the expertise is genuine and demonstrable.

4. **Local relevance trumps broad keyword targeting.** Small businesses should optimize for the geographic areas they serve, not for broad national keywords. "Emergency plumber Sacramento" is a better target than "plumber" because the intent is local, the competition is manageable, and the searcher is likely a potential customer. The video recommends creating content that naturally references the areas you serve.

5. **Reviews are a ranking factor.** The video confirms what local SEO practitioners have long observed: the quantity, quality, and recency of Google reviews influence local search rankings. Google encourages businesses to ask customers for reviews, respond to all reviews (positive and negative), and maintain a consistent flow of new reviews over time.

## What Small Businesses Should Ignore

Equally valuable is the video's guidance on what small businesses should not worry about. Advanced technical SEO like crawl budget optimization, hreflang implementation, and complex structured data schemas are not relevant for most small business sites. Link building campaigns that target high-authority sites are typically cost-prohibitive and unnecessary when local signals are strong.

The video also pushes back on the common small business habit of obsessing over a single keyword ranking. Position tracking for one or two vanity keywords provides a misleading picture of search performance. A small business is better served by monitoring overall organic traffic, phone calls, and direction requests from Google Business Profile, and contact form submissions.

## The Content Approach

For content creation, the video recommends a quality-over-quantity strategy. Rather than publishing weekly blog posts on topics tangentially related to your business, create fewer pieces of comprehensive, expert-level content that address the specific questions your customers ask. A single, thorough guide to "how to choose a kitchen remodel contractor in Sacramento" is worth more than 50 thin blog posts about general home improvement trends.

This advice aligns with how AI-powered search systems work. AI Overviews and LLM-based search engines preferentially cite content that provides specific, expert answers to well-defined questions. Small businesses that create this kind of content are well-positioned to earn AI citations alongside traditional search traffic.
