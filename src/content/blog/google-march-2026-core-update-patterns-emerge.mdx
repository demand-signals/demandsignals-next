The [Google March 2026 Core Update](/blog/google-march-2026-core-update-begins), which began rolling out on March 13th, has passed its midpoint. With ten days of ranking data now available, patterns are emerging that are clear enough to act on — with the caveat that final positions will not stabilize until the rollout completes, likely around March 27th.

## The Big Winners

### Local Businesses with Strong Entity Signals

Local businesses that have invested in consistent NAP data, active [Google Business Profile management](/demand-generation/gbp-admin), verified reviews, and service-area pages are seeing average ranking improvements of 18% across their tracked keywords. This is a meaningful gain that will translate directly to traffic and leads.

The pattern is clear: Google is rewarding businesses that demonstrate real-world existence and local relevance through multiple corroborating signals. A business with a verified GBP, 50+ recent reviews, service-area pages for each location served, and consistent citations across directories is receiving a trust boost that generic content sites cannot match.

### Sites with Structured FAQ Content

Pages with properly implemented FAQ sections using FAQPage schema markup are seeing an average improvement of 12% in ranking position. More significantly, these pages are showing increased appearance in AI Overviews — suggesting that the March 2026 update includes refinements to how Google selects sources for AI-generated answers.

This validates the approach we have been implementing across all client sites: unique, page-specific FAQ sections with FAQPage schema on every service page, category page, and location page. The FAQ content serves triple duty — traditional SEO ranking signals, AI Overview source selection, and [LLM citation optimization](/demand-generation/geo-aeo-llm-optimization).

### Expert-Authored Content with Clear Attribution

Content with clear author bylines, linked author bios demonstrating relevant expertise, and transparent publication dates is outperforming anonymous or vaguely attributed content. This is the E-E-A-T emphasis that Google has been signaling for two years, now being enforced with more precision.

## The Big Losers

### AI Content Farms

Sites that published high volumes of AI-generated content without editorial oversight are seeing average traffic declines of 47%. These are the operations that used AI to produce hundreds or thousands of thin articles targeting long-tail keywords — the "scale without quality" approach that characterized the worst of AI content strategies in 2024-2025.

Important distinction: this is not a penalty on AI-generated content broadly. Sites using AI content generation with editorial oversight, expert review, and genuine topical authority are performing well. The penalty is on content that was generated at scale without quality controls — regardless of whether it was generated by AI or by low-cost human writers following a template.

### Thin Directory and Aggregator Sites

Generic directory sites and content aggregators that add little value beyond collecting information from other sources are declining. Google's AI Overviews can synthesize this information directly, reducing the need for intermediary aggregation sites.

### Sites with Poor Technical Health

Sites with slow load times, poor mobile experience, broken internal links, and outdated security configurations are seeing amplified negative effects during this update. Technical health has always been a ranking factor, but this update appears to give it increased weight — possibly because Google views technical investment as a proxy for overall site quality and active management.

## What This Means for Your Strategy

### If You Are Up

Maintain your momentum. Continue publishing quality content, managing your reviews, and strengthening your local signals. Ranking gains during core updates are not permanent — they require ongoing investment to maintain.

### If You Are Down

Do not make reactive changes during the rollout. Wait for the update to complete, then conduct a thorough analysis of which pages and keywords were affected. The patterns above give you a roadmap for recovery:

1. Audit your content for quality and accuracy — remove or substantially improve thin content
2. Strengthen E-E-A-T signals — author bios, credentials, about page, verifiable business information
3. Implement FAQ schema on key pages
4. Invest in [local SEO fundamentals](/demand-generation/local-seo) — GBP optimization, citation consistency, review management

### If You Are Flat

Being flat during a core update is actually a positive signal. It means your site's quality signals are solid enough to weather algorithmic changes. Use this stability as a foundation to implement improvements that will position you for gains in the next update cycle.
