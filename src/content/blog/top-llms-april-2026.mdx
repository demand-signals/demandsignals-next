Every month, we pull real-world usage data from [OpenRouter](https://openrouter.ai/rankings) — a platform that routes millions of API calls across every major LLM provider — and rank the models that developers and businesses are actually using. Not benchmarks. Not press releases. Real tokens, real money, real usage.

Here's who's winning in April 2026.

## The Top 10

### 1. Claude Sonnet 4 (Anthropic)

**The undisputed usage leader.** Claude Sonnet 4 has held the #1 spot for weeks now, and it's not close. Developers love the balance of intelligence, speed, and price. At $0.003 per 1K input tokens and $0.015 per 1K output tokens, it's the sweet spot — smart enough for complex coding and analysis, fast enough for production, cheap enough to scale.

**Why it wins:** Best-in-class coding ability, strong instruction following, massive 200K context window. The model most developers reach for when they need something that "just works."

### 2. Gemini 2.5 Flash (Google)

**The speed king.** Google's Flash models have carved out a massive niche by being absurdly fast and dirt cheap. Gemini 2.5 Flash processes tokens at rates that make other models look like they're running on dial-up, and the quality is surprisingly good for the price point.

**Why it's here:** When you need to process thousands of API calls per minute — customer support bots, content classification, real-time analysis — Flash is the model you use. Speed over depth.

### 3. Gemini 2.0 Flash (Google)

**The reliable workhorse.** Even with 2.5 Flash available, the 2.0 version maintains massive usage. Many production systems haven't migrated yet (if it ain't broke...), and some developers prefer its slightly different behavior profile for specific tasks.

**Why it persists:** Production inertia is real. Switching models mid-deployment is risky. The 2.0 Flash has proven reliability at scale.

### 4. DeepSeek Chat V3.1 (DeepSeek)

**The dark horse.** DeepSeek came out of nowhere in late 2025 and has been climbing steadily ever since. V3.1 offers performance that rivals Claude and GPT at a fraction of the cost. For price-sensitive applications, it's becoming the default choice.

**Why it's climbing:** Open weights, aggressive pricing, and surprisingly strong reasoning. DeepSeek proved you don't need a $100B war chest to build a competitive LLM.

### 5. Gemini 2.5 Pro (Google)

**Google's premium play.** When you need Google's best thinking — complex reasoning, long document analysis, multimodal tasks — 2.5 Pro is the model. It's more expensive than Flash but significantly more capable for tasks that require depth over speed.

**Why it matters:** The model Google uses to compete directly with Claude Opus and GPT-4o on quality benchmarks.

### 6. Grok Code Fast (xAI)

**The new kid.** Elon's xAI team has been shipping fast, and Grok Code Fast is specifically optimized for programming tasks. It's gaining traction in the developer tools space, particularly for code completion and generation.

**Why it's here:** Aggressive pricing, strong code performance, and the curiosity factor. Developers are testing it against Sonnet for coding tasks.

### 7. Qwen 3 Coder 480B (Alibaba)

**The open-source giant.** At 480 billion parameters, Qwen 3 Coder is one of the largest open-weight coding models available. Running it requires serious infrastructure, but for organizations that want to self-host their AI coding assistant, it's a compelling option.

**Why it's notable:** Proves that open-source models can compete at the frontier. Available through OpenRouter for those who don't want to run 480B parameters on their own GPUs.

### 8. DeepSeek R1 (DeepSeek)

**The reasoner.** While DeepSeek Chat handles general conversation, R1 is their chain-of-thought reasoning model — similar to OpenAI's o1. For math, logic, and complex multi-step problems, R1 punches well above its weight class.

**Why it's growing:** Reasoning models are becoming essential for agent workflows. R1 offers o1-class thinking at DeepSeek prices.

### 9. Claude 3.7 Sonnet (Anthropic)

**The legacy champion.** Despite Sonnet 4 being available, the 3.7 version maintains significant usage. Some production systems are locked to it, and some users genuinely prefer its behavior for certain tasks.

**Why it's still here:** Model version loyalty is real. Some fine-tuned prompts work better with 3.7's specific quirks. Migration takes time.

### 10. Claude Opus 4 (Anthropic)

**The thinking machine.** When you need the absolute best reasoning, longest context, and deepest analysis, Opus is the model. It's expensive ($0.015 input, $0.075 output) but for high-stakes decisions — legal analysis, complex debugging, strategic planning — nothing else comes close.

**Why it rounds out the top 10:** Not used for bulk processing, but essential for the moments when getting it right matters more than getting it fast.

### The OpenAI Story

The elephant in the room: OpenAI at ~4% on OpenRouter. This doesn't mean OpenAI is dying — they still have massive direct API usage and ChatGPT consumer traffic. But in the developer API market (which is what OpenRouter measures), they've lost significant ground. Claude took the coding crown, Gemini Flash took the speed crown, and DeepSeek took the price crown. OpenAI's models are good at everything but best at nothing — a dangerous position in a market that's fragmenting into specialties.

## What This Means for Your Business

If you're building AI-powered products or using AI in your operations, here are the takeaways:

**Don't bet on one provider.** The rankings shift monthly. Build your systems to be model-agnostic where possible. OpenRouter itself is a great abstraction layer for this.

**Price matters more than benchmarks.** DeepSeek's rise proves that "good enough at 1/10th the price" beats "slightly better at premium cost" for most applications.

**Speed and cost determine scale.** The top 3 is dominated by models that are fast and affordable. Intelligence is table stakes — every top-10 model is "smart enough" for most business tasks. The differentiator is how many queries you can run per dollar.

**Reasoning models are a separate category.** DeepSeek R1 and Claude Opus aren't competing with Flash models — they serve different purposes. The market is bifurcating into "fast and cheap" for throughput and "slow and smart" for complex decisions.
