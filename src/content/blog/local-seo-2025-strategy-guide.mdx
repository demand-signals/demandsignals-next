If you are running the same local SEO strategy in 2025 that you ran in 2023, you are losing ground. Not because the old tactics stopped working entirely — some of them still contribute — but because the landscape has shifted enough that strategies optimized for two years ago leave significant traffic and leads on the table.

Here is what changed, what works now, and what you should stop spending time on.

## What Changed in Local Search

### AI Overviews Replaced Some of Your Best Rankings

Google's AI Overviews now appear on 30-40% of local service queries. When an AI Overview appears, it pushes the traditional map pack and organic results below the fold. The click-through rate for the #1 organic position drops by 25-40% when an AI Overview is present above it.

This means that even if your SEO strategy successfully ranks you #1 for a valuable keyword, you may be getting significantly fewer clicks than that position used to deliver. The solution is not to abandon organic ranking — it still matters — but to also optimize for inclusion in AI Overviews.

### Alternative Search Platforms Are Real

ChatGPT Search (now free for all users), Perplexity, and other AI-powered search tools are capturing a growing share of local service queries. These platforms do not use traditional ranking algorithms. They synthesize answers from web content, reviews, and business data, citing two to four businesses by name.

A local business that ranks #1 on Google but is never cited by ChatGPT or Perplexity is invisible to a fast-growing segment of searchers. The 2025 strategy must address all discovery channels, not just Google organic.

### Google Business Profile Became More Important

Google has been steadily increasing the influence of Google Business Profile (GBP) signals on local search results. In 2025, GBP optimization is not a "nice to have" — it is the single highest-ROI activity in local SEO.

Businesses with complete, actively managed GBP profiles — including regular posts, updated hours, comprehensive service descriptions, Q&A responses, and consistent review activity — rank meaningfully higher than businesses with set-and-forget profiles.

## What Works Now: The 2025 Local SEO Playbook

### Foundation: Google Business Profile Excellence

Your GBP profile should be treated as a living marketing asset, not a directory listing. The optimization checklist:

**Complete every field.** Every service you offer should be listed. Every product should be added if applicable. Your business description should use all 750 characters. Categories should include your primary category and all relevant secondary categories.

**Post weekly.** GBP posts signal active management and provide fresh content signals. Each post should include a relevant image, a brief description of a service or update, and a call to action. Consistency matters more than perfection.

**Respond to every review.** Every review — positive, negative, and neutral — should receive a personalized response within 48 hours. Responses should reference the specific service mentioned, include location keywords naturally, and demonstrate engagement.

**Use the Q&A feature proactively.** Add common questions and answers to your GBP profile before customers ask them. This provides structured information that both Google and AI systems can use.

**Add photos regularly.** Businesses with 100+ photos receive 520% more calls than businesses with fewer than 10 photos, according to Google's own data. Upload professional photos of your team, your work, and your location at least monthly.

### Layer 2: Website Content Depth

Every service you offer needs a dedicated page with substantial content. The minimum standard in 2025 is 1,200+ words of genuine, useful content per service page. Each page should include:

- Clear description of the service and your approach
- Process overview from inquiry to completion
- Common questions answered directly (with FAQPage schema)
- Location-specific details (service areas, local considerations)
- Differentiators explained concretely, not with generic claims

For businesses serving multiple cities, each significant city should have a dedicated location page with content specific to that area — not just a template with the city name swapped in. Google's quality systems have become quite effective at identifying thin, templated location pages.

### Layer 3: Review Velocity and Quality

Reviews in 2025 serve triple duty: they influence Google rankings, they influence AI Overview citation, and they influence ChatGPT and Perplexity recommendations. The businesses with the strongest review profiles win across all three channels.

**Target 20+ new reviews per month** for active businesses. The exact number depends on your industry and market size, but the key is consistent velocity rather than periodic bursts.

**Coach customers toward specific reviews.** When requesting reviews, ask customers to mention the specific service they received, the location, and a specific positive outcome. "Would you mind mentioning the kitchen remodel and how the timeline worked out?" produces dramatically more valuable review content than a generic review request.

**Respond to every review with specificity.** Your responses should reference the service, the location, and the outcome. This creates keyword-rich content on your GBP profile that signals relevance for those terms.

### Layer 4: Structured Data and Technical SEO

Schema markup is the bridge between your content and AI systems. Implement at minimum:

- **LocalBusiness** schema on your homepage
- **Service** schema on every service page
- **FAQPage** schema on every page with FAQ content
- **BreadcrumbList** schema for navigation clarity
- **AggregateRating** schema for your review scores

Beyond schema, the technical fundamentals still matter: Core Web Vitals scores, mobile usability, HTTPS, clean URL structure, proper internal linking, and XML sitemap accuracy. These are not exciting, but they are the foundation everything else builds on.

### Layer 5: GEO and AEO Optimization

This is the new layer that did not exist in the 2023 playbook. Generative Engine Optimization and Answer Engine Optimization address AI-powered discovery specifically:

**Implement llms.txt.** Provide AI models with a structured summary of your business at yourdomain.com/llms.txt.

**Build content for extraction.** Structure your content so AI systems can extract direct answers to specific questions. Lead with the answer, then provide context and detail.

**Monitor AI citations.** Regularly check whether your business appears in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview results for your target keywords. Track changes over time.

**Maintain citation consistency.** Your NAP (name, address, phone) must be identical across every platform. Inconsistencies reduce AI citation confidence.

## What to Stop Doing

**Stop building low-quality backlinks.** The era of quantity over quality in link building is over. A few genuine local backlinks from chambers of commerce, industry associations, and local news coverage are worth more than dozens of directory links from irrelevant sites.

**Stop creating thin location pages.** Templated pages that swap city names are not just ineffective — they can trigger quality penalties. Every location page needs genuinely unique content.

**Stop ignoring alternative search platforms.** If you are only monitoring Google rankings and not tracking AI search citations, you are blind to a growing channel.

**Stop treating SEO as a one-time project.** The frequency of Google's algorithm updates (four core updates in 2024 alone) and the emergence of AI search mean that SEO requires continuous attention, not periodic campaigns.

## What This Means for Your Business

Local SEO in 2025 is a three-channel game: Google organic, Google AI Overviews, and alternative AI search platforms. The businesses that optimize across all three channels will capture the most customers. The businesses that focus only on one channel will see their market share erode as search behavior diversifies.

The good news is that the core investments — content quality, review management, structured data, and consistent online presence — benefit all three channels simultaneously. Building the foundation right means you are competitive everywhere, not just on Google.

The businesses that execute this playbook now will have compounding advantages as AI search continues to grow. The window for establishing authority is open, and it rewards early movers disproportionately.
