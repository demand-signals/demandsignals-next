### New This Month

**Claude Sonnet 4.6** (Jan 28) — Anthropic's mid-cycle upgrade slots in right behind 3.5 Sonnet at the same price point. Better instruction following, stronger agentic tool use, and noticeably improved code editing. Developers are migrating fast — the question isn't *if* it overtakes 3.5, but *when*.

**Claude Opus 4.6** (Jan 28) — The heavy hitter. Opus pricing keeps it niche, but for complex reasoning chains, legal analysis, and multi-step research tasks, nothing else comes close. Enterprise adoption is strong.

**Gemini 3 Flash** (Jan 15, GA) — Google's next-gen speed model graduates from preview to general availability. Same absurd throughput as the preview, now with production SLAs. Already eating Gemini 2.0 Flash's volume from the bottom.

**Qwen3 Max Thinking** (Jan 22) — Alibaba's reasoning-first model enters the top 10 on price alone. At $0.0002/1K input tokens, it's the cheapest thinking model on the market. Quality lags behind Claude and GPT for English tasks, but it's dominant for Chinese-language workloads.

### The Story

February's headline: **Anthropic owns three of the top ten spots.** Claude 3.5 Sonnet holds the crown for the second consecutive month, but Claude Sonnet 4.6 is climbing so fast that the two might swap positions by March. Developers with existing 3.5 Sonnet integrations are cautious — the models share identical pricing, but 4.6's behavioral differences require testing.

GPT-5.2 is the month's biggest mover, jumping three spots to #4 as developers finish evaluating and start committing production traffic. OpenAI's pricing undercut worked.

Google's Flash lineup now holds two top-10 spots. The combined Flash volume (2.0 + 3) makes Google the clear leader in high-throughput, low-cost inference. If you're processing millions of documents, you're probably running Flash.

GPT-4o drops to #7 — its first time outside the top 5. The writing is on the wall for last-gen flagships.
