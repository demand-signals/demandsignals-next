Google confirmed yesterday that the March 2026 Core Update has begun rolling out. This is the first core update of 2026, following the [December 2025 core update](/blog/google-december-2025-core-update-devastation) that caused significant ranking disruption across multiple industries.

Search volatility trackers are already showing elevated movement. Semrush's Sensor hit 8.4 within the first 24 hours — well above the 5.0 baseline that indicates normal fluctuation. Approximately 42% of tracked sites are showing meaningful ranking changes, though it is too early to identify definitive patterns.

## Early Signals: AI Content Quality

The most credible early analysis — and it is genuinely early, so treat all pattern identification with appropriate skepticism — suggests this update includes refined signals for evaluating AI-generated content quality. Not penalizing AI content broadly, but distinguishing between AI content that provides genuine value and AI content that is mass-produced without editorial oversight.

This aligns with Google's stated direction over the past year. The search quality team has repeatedly emphasized that the method of content creation (human vs. AI) is less important than the quality, accuracy, and usefulness of the output. The March 2026 update appears to operationalize this principle with more sophisticated quality signals.

### What "AI Content Quality Signals" Likely Means

Based on Google's published guidance and the patterns emerging in early ranking data:

**Factual accuracy** — AI-generated content that contains demonstrably incorrect claims is being devalued more aggressively. This penalizes operations that publish AI output without fact-checking.

**Topical depth** — Surface-level content that covers a topic broadly but without genuine expertise is losing ground to content that demonstrates deep, specific knowledge. A 600-word overview of "how HVAC systems work" loses to a 1,200-word piece that explains the specific maintenance requirements of a Carrier Infinity system in Sacramento's climate.

**Entity consistency** — Content associated with established, consistent web entities (businesses with verified NAP data, consistent branding, cross-platform presence) appears to be receiving a ranking boost. This rewards businesses that have invested in [local SEO](/demand-generation/local-seo) and consistent online presence management.

## What to Do Right Now

The update is rolling out, which means changes you make today will not affect your rankings during this update cycle. Core updates evaluate content as it existed when the update process began. But there are productive actions to take:

### 1. Document Your Rankings

Take snapshots of your current rankings for all tracked keywords. You need a baseline to measure the update's impact when the rollout completes in approximately two weeks.

### 2. Audit Your AI Content Pipeline

If you are publishing AI-generated content, this is the moment to verify your editorial process. At Demand Signals, our [AI content generation](/content-social/ai-content-generation) workflow includes human editorial review for every piece — not because AI output is inherently unreliable, but because editorial review is the quality signal that distinguishes professional AI-assisted content from mass-produced AI spam.

### 3. Strengthen E-E-A-T Signals

Ensure your website clearly communicates Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Author bylines with credentials. About pages with verifiable business information. Service pages with specific, experience-based detail rather than generic descriptions.

### 4. Review Your GEO/AEO Positioning

Core updates often change which sites get cited in AI Overviews. If you have invested in [GEO and AEO optimization](/demand-generation/geo-aeo-llm-optimization), monitor your AI Overview citation rates alongside your traditional rankings as the update rolls out.

## Historical Context

For perspective, here is how recent core updates have played out:

- **December 2025:** Heavy volatility, 2-3 week rollout, significant impact on sites with thin content and poor E-E-A-T signals
- **June 2025:** Moderate impact, focused on local search quality
- **March 2025:** The largest core update of 2025, with widespread ranking changes across industries

The March 2026 update is shaping up to be similarly significant. We will publish follow-up analysis as the rollout progresses and clearer patterns emerge.
