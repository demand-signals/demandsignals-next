If you have been paying attention to how people find businesses online, you have noticed that the landscape has fractured. Google organic results are not the only game anymore. AI Overviews, ChatGPT search, Perplexity, and voice assistants all represent distinct discovery channels — and each one requires a different optimization approach.

SEO, GEO, and AEO are three acronyms that describe three different strategies for three different discovery environments. Most businesses are only doing one of them. That is a problem.

## What Is SEO?

**Search Engine Optimization** is the practice of optimizing your website and content to rank higher in traditional search engine results. This is the strategy most businesses know. It encompasses technical optimization (site speed, mobile-friendliness, crawlability), on-page optimization (keywords, meta tags, headings, content quality), and off-page optimization (backlinks, brand mentions, domain authority).

SEO targets Google's organic search results — the ten blue links. When someone searches "plumber near me" and your business appears in the results or the local pack, that is SEO at work.

**SEO has been around for 25+ years.** The tactics have evolved, but the core principle remains: make your content the best answer to the search query, and make your site technically sound enough for search engines to find, crawl, and index it.

## What Is GEO?

**Generative Engine Optimization** is the practice of optimizing your content to appear in AI-generated search results, particularly Google's AI Overviews. When someone searches a question and Google generates a synthesized answer at the top of the results page, the sources cited in that answer got there through GEO.

GEO is different from SEO because AI Overviews do not simply pull the top-ranking result. They synthesize information from multiple sources, favoring content that is:

- **Structured and clear** — organized with headers, lists, and direct answers
- **Authoritative** — backed by data, expert credentials, and citations
- **Specific** — addresses the exact nuance of the query rather than covering a broad topic
- **Schema-enriched** — uses structured data that AI systems can parse programmatically

[GEO optimization](/demand-generation/geo-aeo-llm-optimization) requires a different content strategy than traditional SEO. You are not just competing for a ranking position — you are competing to be the source that an AI model chooses to cite in its synthesized answer.

## What Is AEO?

**AI Engine Optimization** is the practice of optimizing your business information and content to be accurately represented and cited by AI assistants — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and others. When someone asks an AI assistant "who is the best web developer in Sacramento?" and your business is mentioned in the response, that is AEO working.

AEO focuses on:

- **llms.txt files** — machine-readable summaries of your business and services that AI models can parse
- **Structured data and schema markup** — making your business information unambiguous for AI systems
- **Consistent entity representation** — ensuring your business name, services, and expertise appear consistently across the sources AI models reference
- **Citation-worthy content** — creating content with specific data points, unique frameworks, and definitive answers that AI systems are likely to cite

AEO is the newest of the three and the least understood by most businesses. It is also the fastest-growing discovery channel.

## How They Differ: A Direct Comparison

| Dimension | SEO | GEO | AEO |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Target | Google organic results | AI Overviews (Google) | AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.) |
| What ranks | Web pages | Content citations | Entity mentions and citations |
| Key signals | Backlinks, keywords, page speed | Structure, authority, specificity | Consistency, schema, llms.txt |
| Measurement | Rankings, organic traffic | AI Overview appearances | AI citation monitoring |
| Timeframe | Weeks to months | Days to weeks | Weeks to months |
| Maturity | 25+ years | 2 years | 1 year |

## When Does Each One Matter?

### SEO matters when:
- You need consistent, measurable organic traffic
- Your customers search for your services on Google
- You compete for local pack visibility
- You want long-term compound traffic growth

### GEO matters when:
- Your target queries trigger AI Overviews (now 48%+ of all searches)
- You want to be cited as the authoritative source in AI-generated answers
- Your competitors are appearing in AI Overviews and you are not
- You create content that answers specific questions

### AEO matters when:
- Your customers use AI assistants to research services
- You want to be recommended by ChatGPT, Perplexity, or other AI tools
- You operate in a market where AI-referred traffic is growing
- You want to future-proof your discoverability as AI adoption accelerates

## Why You Probably Need All Three

Here is the reality most businesses miss: these three strategies are complementary, not competing. A business that only does SEO is invisible to AI search users. A business that only focuses on AEO misses the 90%+ of searches that still happen on Google. And GEO without solid SEO foundations rarely works — Google's AI Overviews still favor authoritative sites with strong organic signals.

The three-layer approach works because each strategy reinforces the others:

1. **SEO builds the foundation** — your site needs to be technically sound, well-structured, and authoritative for any optimization strategy to work
2. **GEO extends your reach** — structured, citation-worthy content that performs in AI Overviews also tends to rank well organically
3. **AEO captures emerging channels** — the llms.txt files, consistent schema, and entity-rich content that AI assistants reference also strengthen your SEO and GEO signals

The businesses gaining the most visibility right now are running [integrated optimization strategies](/demand-generation/geo-aeo-llm-optimization) across all three layers simultaneously.

## How to Get Started

**If you are doing nothing:** Start with SEO fundamentals. Fix your technical foundation, optimize your Google Business Profile, and create quality content targeting your core service queries.

**If you already have solid SEO:** Add GEO by restructuring your content for AI Overview citation. Add FAQ sections, use clear headings, include specific data points, and implement comprehensive schema markup.

**If you have SEO and GEO covered:** Layer in AEO by creating an llms.txt file, ensuring entity consistency across all platforms, and monitoring how AI assistants represent your business.

These are not emerging trends — they are current reality.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I do GEO and AEO without traditional SEO?
Technically, but it is not advisable. SEO provides the technical and authority foundation that makes GEO and AEO effective. A site with poor Core Web Vitals, no schema markup, and thin content will struggle with AI optimization regardless of how many llms.txt files you create.

### How do I know if my business is appearing in AI Overviews?
Search for your core service queries on Google and check whether an AI Overview appears. If it does, check whether your business or content is cited. Tools like Semrush and specialized GEO monitoring platforms can track this at scale. You can also search for your business name and services on ChatGPT and Perplexity to assess your AEO visibility.

### How long does it take to see results from GEO and AEO optimization?
GEO results can appear within days to weeks as Google re-crawls your structured content. AEO results depend on when AI models update their knowledge bases — typically weeks to months. SEO remains the slowest to show results but delivers the most consistent long-term traffic.

### Is GEO the same thing as AEO?
No. GEO specifically targets Google's AI Overviews — the AI-generated answers that appear at the top of Google search results. AEO targets standalone AI assistants like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. The optimization tactics overlap but are distinct because the ranking signals differ between Google's AI system and other AI platforms.
