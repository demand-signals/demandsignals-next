Google confirmed the March 2025 core update on March 13th, marking the first broad core algorithm change of the year. The rollout completed on March 27th, and the data from the first two weeks tells a clear story: this update is a continuation of Google's multi-year campaign to reward content depth and punish surface-level optimization.

## What the Data Shows

Across the tracking tools we monitor — Semrush Sensor, Mozcast, and manual SERP audits for our client categories — the volatility signature for this update is distinctive. Unlike the November 2024 update, which broadly impacted sites across categories, the March 2025 update shows concentrated impact in specific verticals.

**Professional services** (legal, accounting, consulting) saw the most significant ranking shifts. Sites that had been ranking on domain authority alone — thin service pages with minimal content, no FAQ sections, and no structured data — dropped an average of 8-12 positions for their primary keywords.

**Local service businesses** (HVAC, plumbing, dental, auto repair) experienced a more nuanced shift. Sites with strong Google Business Profiles and review velocity held steady or gained. Sites that had been relying exclusively on organic content without local entity signals lost ground.

**E-commerce and affiliate sites** continued the downward trend that started with the September 2023 helpful content update. Programmatically generated product comparison pages without original analysis are being systematically devalued.

## The Three Signals That Matter

Analyzing the winners and losers across our client portfolio and the broader SERP landscape, three quality signals appear to be receiving increased weight in this update.

### 1. Content Comprehensiveness Per Page

The update is rewarding pages that thoroughly cover their topic rather than sites that spread thin content across many pages. A single 2,500-word service page with FAQ schema, detailed process descriptions, and specific examples is outperforming a site with ten 300-word pages targeting keyword variations of the same topic.

This is a direct consequence of AI Overview expansion. Google's AI models need comprehensive source pages to generate accurate overviews. Pages that provide that depth are being elevated as preferred sources.

### 2. Entity Consistency Across the Web

Businesses with consistent NAP (name, address, phone) data across directories, consistent service descriptions across platforms, and a well-maintained Google Business Profile are being rewarded. This is not new, but the signal weight appears to have increased.

The likely mechanism: as Google's AI models pull from multiple sources to generate overviews and local results, inconsistent entity data creates conflicting signals that reduce the model's confidence in recommending that business.

### 3. Freshness of Expertise Signals

Stale content is losing ground to fresh content. Sites that published their service pages in 2021 and have not updated them since are dropping behind competitors who refresh content quarterly with updated statistics, new case studies, and current industry context.

This freshness signal extends beyond content dates. Review recency, social media activity, and recent backlinks all appear to contribute to a composite freshness score that the update weights more heavily than previous iterations.

## What Did Not Change

Despite the volatility, several factors remained stable:

**Backlink authority** still matters. Sites with strong backlink profiles did not lose rankings even if their on-page content was not optimal. But backlinks alone are no longer sufficient to hold positions against competitors with better content.

**Technical SEO fundamentals** — page speed, mobile responsiveness, Core Web Vitals — did not show significant re-weighting in this update. Sites that were already meeting thresholds saw no additional benefit from further optimization.

**Domain age** continues to carry implicit authority, but new domains with strong content are closing the gap faster than in previous years. We observed several sub-two-year-old domains outranking established competitors in local service categories.

## The AI Overview Connection

What makes this update particularly significant is its timing relative to Google's continued expansion of AI Overviews. The March update increased AI Overview triggers by an estimated 18% across commercial and informational queries.

This creates a compounding effect: the update both elevates content that AI models prefer to cite AND increases the number of queries where AI Overviews appear. Businesses that are optimized for both traditional ranking and AI citation are seeing double benefits. Businesses optimized for neither are seeing compound losses.

## Recovery Strategy

If your site lost rankings in this update, the recovery path is straightforward but requires genuine effort:

**Audit your content depth.** Every service page should have a minimum of 1,500 words of substantive content, a FAQ section with five or more questions, and appropriate schema markup. Thin pages need to be consolidated or expanded.

**Fix your entity consistency.** Run a citation audit across all major directories and platforms. Every mention of your business should have identical core information. A [local SEO strategy](/demand-generation/local-seo) that includes ongoing citation management is no longer optional.

**Refresh your content.** Update statistics, add recent examples, and ensure every page reflects current industry conditions. Add publication dates and "last updated" timestamps to signal freshness to both users and crawlers.

**Build review velocity.** If your Google Business Profile has fewer than 50 reviews or your last review is more than 30 days old, review generation should be an immediate priority.

## What This Means for Your Business

The March 2025 core update is not an isolated event — it is another step in Google's systematic elevation of comprehensive, authoritative, and fresh content. Each successive update makes it harder to rank on technical SEO tricks and easier to rank on genuine expertise and content depth.

Businesses that have invested in [comprehensive demand generation systems](/demand-generation/systems) — content depth, entity consistency, review velocity, and structured data — are compounding their advantage with every update. Businesses that are still running a 2020 SEO playbook are falling further behind with each cycle.

The gap between these two groups is widening, and it is becoming increasingly expensive to close. The best time to start building a modern search presence was two years ago. The second-best time is today.
